# AEX-1 "Avalanche Express"  
### Strategic Heavy Bomber Concept Design

## Project Overview

The AEX-1 “Avalanche Express” was a semester-long aerospace engineering team project focused on designing a next-generation American heavy strategic bomber. 

The concept was inspired by the payload scale and mission capability of the C-5 Galaxy, while using the structural proportions and configuration philosophy of the Airbus A380 as a baseline reference platform.

The goal was to redesign and repurpose a super-heavy aircraft platform into a modernized military bomber capable of:

- Carrying extremely large payloads (including heavy armored military vehicles)
- Sustaining high structural load factors
- Achieving long-range strategic mission profiles
- Withstanding significant aerodynamic and structural stress under combat conditions

This project culminated in a 100+ page technical report covering aerodynamic analysis, structural calculations, materials selection, performance metrics, and mission feasibility.

### Aerodynamics
- Lift and drag estimation
- Wing area sizing

- Load factor (G-force) analysis

### Structures
- Composite material selection for high strength-to-weight ratio
- Structural reinforcement for extreme payload capacity
- Bending moment and stress analysis
- Fatigue and load distribution concepts

### Performance & Mission Analysis
- Payload capability (including heavy armored military vehicles)
- Range and endurance estimation
- Cruise performance modeling
- Structural limits under aggressive maneuvering
- Strategic mission deployment scenarios

## Design Philosophy

The AEX-1 was designed to bridge the gap between heavy transport capability and strategic bombing functionality.

Key design goals:

- Massive internal payload volume
- Reinforced wing structure for extreme loading
- Efficient high-altitude cruise
- Structural survivability under high G-load scenarios
- Modular internal configuration for adaptable mission profiles
