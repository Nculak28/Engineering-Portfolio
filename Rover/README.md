# Rover Project

## Project Overview

This project involved designing, manufacturing, and competing with a Mars rover-style robot as part of a five-person engineering team. The objective of the competition was to transfer balls over a central wall into the opposing team's zone within a one-minute match. The team with the most balls remaining on their side at the end of the match lost.

The rover was fully remote-controlled and required mechanical design, manufacturing, and control system integration.

## Mechanical Design

The rover featured:

- Four-wheel drive configuration  
- Tank-style tread layout for improved traction  
- Front-mounted scooper mechanism  
- Dual L-shaped lifting arms with integrated sliding scoop functionality  

### Scooper Mechanism

The scooper assembly was the key competitive feature of the rover.

The L-shaped arms rotated upward about bearing-supported pivot points. As the arms lifted, the scoop was designed to slide along the arms due to gravity and geometric alignment. This sliding action allowed controlled ball release over the wall without requiring an additional actuator. The right side L shaped arm had a chain and sprocket mechanism to pull the arms up.

This passive mechanical solution:

- Reduced system complexity  
- Simplified control logic  
- Improved reliability during competition

## Manufacturing Process

The rover was fabricated using:

- Laser-cut plastic structural components  
- Select sheet metal reinforcement  
- Bearings at arm pivot locations  `
- Rubber band stabilization system to maintain arm tension and alignment  

All components were modeled in SolidWorks and exported as STL files for laser cutting. This enabled:

- High dimensional accuracy  
- Lightweight structural design  
- Rapid iteration during prototyping  

---

## Control System Integration

Two team members focused on coding and remote operation of the rover. The drive system and scooper actuation were controlled via remote interface.

Because the scooper utilized a gravity-assisted sliding mechanism, mechanical simplicity reduced the programming burden and improved consistency during timed matches.

---

## Engineering Challenges & Solutions

### Controlled Ball Release
**Challenge:** Deliver balls over the wall without complex multi-actuator systems.  
**Solution:** Designed a gravity-assisted sliding scoop integrated into rotating L-shaped arms.

### Structural Rigidity
**Challenge:** Maintaining stiffness in laser-cut plastic components under load.  
**Solution:** Reinforced high-stress regions and implemented tension stabilization using elastic bands.

### Smooth Arm Rotation
**Challenge:** Minimizing friction and binding during lifting motion.  
**Solution:** Integrated bearings at pivot points for smooth, repeatable arm movement.
